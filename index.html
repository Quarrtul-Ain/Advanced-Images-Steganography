What is Steganography?

Steganography is the art of hiding information within other non-secret, ordinary-looking files — such as images. Unlike encryption, which scrambles the message but reveals that something is hidden, steganography conceals the existence of the message itself.

How Our Technology Works

We use LSB (Least Significant Bit) steganography to embed your message inside the pixels of an image. For added protection, AES-256 encryption is available, ensuring military-grade security for your secret data.

A Brief History

Steganography dates back to ancient civilizations — from hidden messages in wax tablets to secret ink techniques. It has evolved with technology and today plays a vital role in secure digital communication.
